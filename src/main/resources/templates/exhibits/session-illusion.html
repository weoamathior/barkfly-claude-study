Shared Cookie / Session Illusion

tagline

Why enterprises do this

"It's all one login, right?" The portal and the embedded app are assumed to share one session. Usually they don't — they have separate sessions, separate timeouts, sometimes separate identity providers.

Why it hurts

  • The portal looks logged in while the child app's session has expired.
  • The child silently shows a login screen or a blank frame.
  • The UI keeps showing stale data the user can no longer act on (zombie UI).
  • Logout in one app doesn't log out the other.

BAD — Portal trusts its own session, never checks the child

Portal: —
Child app: not loaded.

BETTER — Explicit child session check, reauth flow, logout broadcast + ack

Portal: —
Child: — (polled via /api/session/status?app=child)

Reset

Scripted walkthrough

  1. BAD: Open portal + child (both logged in). Click Expire child session. The portal still looks fine and the child still shows stale orders.
  2. BAD: Click Do an action in the child — the portal optimistically "succeeds" even though the child session is dead (zombie UI). Logout (portal only) leaves the child session as-is.
  3. BETTER: Start session monitor, then Expire child session: within a couple seconds the monitor detects it and shows a re-authenticate banner.
  4. BETTER: Re-authenticate child clears the banner. Logout everywhere logs out both apps and logs an acknowledgement from each.

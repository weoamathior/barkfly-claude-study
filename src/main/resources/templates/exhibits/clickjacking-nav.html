Zoo / Exhibit #6

Click Interception / Fake Navigation

tagline

Why enterprises do this

To make several apps feel like "one product," the portal intercepts link clicks and swaps content in place — no full page load, no flicker, URL stays put. Looks seamless in the demo.

Why it hurts

  • The URL no longer reflects where you are — bookmarks and sharing break.
  • The back button leaves the whole portal instead of going back one view.
  • Open-in-new-tab / middle-click do nothing (href is #).
  • No breadcrumb / boundary — users don't know which app they're in.

BAD — Intercept every click, swap content, never touch history

You are here: Home (breadcrumb never updates)
Orders · Invoices · Support
Welcome to the portal home.

BETTER — Real links, pushState routes, breadcrumb, working back

You are here: Portal › Home
Orders App · Invoices App · Support App
Welcome to the portal home.

These are real <a href> links — middle-click / "open in new tab" works, and each view pushes a history entry so Back returns one view.

Reset

Scripted walkthrough

  1. BAD: Click Orders, then Invoices — content swaps but the URL and breadcrumb never change. Now click Press browser Back: it leaves the exhibit entirely (no in-app history).
  2. BAD: Try to middle-click / open a link in a new tab — nothing useful happens (the href is #).
  3. BETTER: Click Orders App then Invoices App — the URL gains ?view=… and the breadcrumb updates. Press browser Back returns you to Orders, then Home.
  4. BETTER: Open a link in a new tab — it loads straight to that view (the route owns its URL).
